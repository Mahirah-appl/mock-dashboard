Success Story: Prairie Roots Farm: Growing Beyond the Field

In the heart of rural Alberta near Stettler, Prairie Roots Farm began as a small third-generation grain and cattle operation. When a couple, Jenna and Luke MacDonald returned home from university, they saw potential beyond the traditional farming model. With a passion for local food and community, they began attending County of Stettler agricultural workshops and networking events hosted by the Stettler Board of Trade and local agri-tourism networks.

Through these connections, the MacDonalds discovered a growing interest in farm-to-table products. They started small—offering grass-fed beef boxes and heritage grain flour at the Stettler Farmers' Market. With mentorship from a local agri-food processor and help from a regional business accelerator, they invested in on-site cold storage and a small-scale milling operation. Within three years, Prairie Roots expanded to offer packaged flour, honey, and preserved garden goods under their new brand, “Prairie Pantry.”

Support from local business organizations played a crucial role in their growth. The Stettler Regional Board of Trade helped them develop a business plan and access grant funding for value-added equipment, while Community Futures East Parkland provided startup financing and marketing advice. With these supports, they gained the confidence to scale and diversify their offerings.

Today, Prairie Roots products are sold in farm stores across Central Alberta and through a thriving CSA (Community Supported Agriculture) subscription. The MacDonalds attribute their success not just to innovation, but to the strength of rural relationships: “It started with coffee chats and community meetings,” says Jenna. “The people here want to see each other thrive.”
